Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

1875 - 1950

Iron Man of India

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel (31 October 1875 â€“ 15 December 1950) was an Indian statesman and a key figure in the country's struggle for independence. He served as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India. Patel was instrumental in the integration of over 560 princely states into the Indian Union, and he is often remembered as the "Iron Man of India" for his leadership and determination in unifying the country.[1]

About The Legend

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel played a crucial role in the Indian independence movement alongside Mahatma Gandhi.
  • He was born in Nadiad, British India, and after completing his studies in England, he returned to India and began his legal practice in Gujarat.
  • Patel became a leading figure in the Indian National Congress and was known for his organizational skills and commitment to non-violent resistance.
  • He played a pivotal role in various civil disobedience movements, including the Salt Satyagraha, and was imprisoned several times by British authorities.
  • After India gained independence in 1947, Patel was appointed as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister in the Cabinet of Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru.
  • One of Patel's most significant achievements was the integration of princely states into the Indian Union, which helped shape the political and territorial unity of modern India.
  • He passed away on 15 December 1950, leaving behind a legacy of unity, integrity, and statesmanship.
  • In recognition of his contributions, the tallest statue in the world, the Statue of Unity, was dedicated to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel in Gujarat, India, on 31 October 2018, on his 143rd birth anniversary.
